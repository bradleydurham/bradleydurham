# Beginnings of a site & exploration of new things
Since the early days of the web, I have been interested in creating websites. That has taken many forms over the years. In the early days, it was learning HTML and CSS. Because of work, and the fact that I did not build websites for a living, I lost pace in the early 2000's. 

Fast forward to ~2009, I became energized again. The world was going through a huge financial crisis. I was worried the job I had would evaporate any day. So, I picked back up with learning web design and development. It was the early days of WordPress, and that was quite the buzz back then. So for the better part of 15 years, I was mired in building and maintaining WordPress websites. 

I loved building them, but hated maintaining them. 

Off and on over the years, I have tried to learn javascript. And I failed miserably. Several years ago, I started reading about headless sites and JAMSTACK. Once again, I tried to learn javascript so that I too could ditch WordPress and move on to something newer and more exciting. 

Earlier this year, I gave into peer pressure to try AI. After a brief stint with ChatGPT, I landed with Claude. Claude and specifically, Claude Code, has opened a new world of being able to explore design directions without really knowing javascript. 

After playing around with Claude Code and building my first HTML/CSS/Javascript only site in a really long time, I decided that something with a little bit of modularity was better than brute force. For now, I am trying out Astro as my site builder. So far, I have built static personal sites, but I can't wait to try it on a production site.
